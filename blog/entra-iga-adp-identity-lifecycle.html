How ADP workforce events automatically trigger Joiner / Mover / Leaver workflows in Microsoft Entra Identity Governance — with Aquera and SailPoint as the connector layer.

The notification arrives before the new hire does. Six weeks before Marina Costa Silva's first day as a Senior Platform Engineer, an HR administrator updates her start date in ADP Workforce Now. In a well-governed organization, that single record update quietly sets off a chain reaction across a dozen systems: a Microsoft Entra account is created, security groups are assigned, a Figma workspace seat is provisioned, a GitHub organization invite is queued, and a Temporary Access Pass for passwordless first login is dispatched. Marina walks in on Monday. She can work.

But in most organizations, that chain reaction does not happen automatically. It requires a ticket. It requires someone in IT to notice. It requires coordination between HR, IT, Security, and half a dozen application owners. By the time it is all done, Marina has waited three days for her laptop to unlock and a week for her GitHub access. Meanwhile, Pedro Almeida, who resigned last Tuesday, still has an active Salesforce login.

The gap between how identity should work and how it actually works is one of the most consequential security blind spots in the modern enterprise. This is the problem that Microsoft Entra Identity Governance — paired with HR connectors like Aquera and SailPoint — is designed to close, permanently.

Section 01

The Identity Lifecycle Problem at Scale

Identity governance is, at its core, a matching problem. On one side, you have people — employees who join, change roles, transfer departments, take parental leave, and eventually leave. On the other side, you have resources: hundreds of SaaS applications, cloud environments, file shares, and security groups, each with its own access model. The job of an Identity Governance and Administration (IGA) system is to ensure that every person has exactly the access they need — no more, no less — at every stage of their lifecycle.

In practice, this is extraordinarily hard to do manually. The average enterprise employee today has active accounts in more than 35 SaaS applications. A mid-size company of 3,000 employees might manage over 100,000 individual application accounts across Salesforce, GitHub, Slack, Jira, Figma, AWS, and dozens more. When someone changes departments, their old access should be revoked and new access provisioned — often across 15 or more systems simultaneously. When someone is terminated, every single account must be disabled within hours, not days.

The statistics are sobering. According to identity security research, 58% of organizations take more than 24 hours to fully deprovision a terminated employee. A quarter take more than a week. In that window, former employees retain access to sensitive systems they should never touch again. Insider threats, credential abuse, and accidental data access all exploit exactly this gap — and it is a gap that grows with every new SaaS tool added to the portfolio.

⚠ The Orphaned Account Problem

Industry estimates suggest that 30–50% of SaaS application accounts belong to people who no longer need them — either former employees, contractors, or users who changed roles. Each unused account is a dormant attack vector. A compromised credential for a forgotten Figma editor account can become the entry point for a data exfiltration campaign.

Section 02

ADP Workforce Now: The System of Record for Identity

The solution to the provisioning problem starts with recognizing that HR already has the data. ADP Workforce Now, one of the largest HR management platforms in the world, contains the definitive record of who works at your company: what their role is, which department they belong to, who their manager is, what cost center they're in, and critically — when they start, when they move, and when they leave. Every identity event in the enterprise begins as an HR event.

A hire is a new worker record in ADP. A department transfer is an attribute change on an existing record. A resignation is a status update to workerStatus = Terminated. If your identity infrastructure can listen to these events in real time and react automatically, you have the foundation of a functioning Joiner/Mover/Leaver (JML) lifecycle — the three scenarios that govern virtually all identity activity in an organization.

🟢
Joiner
New hire detected → create identity, provision access packages, send welcome + TAP
🔄
Mover
Dept/role change → swap access packages, delta-provision new apps, revoke old entitlements
🔴
Leaver
Termination event → block sign-in, revoke all tokens, deprovision all apps within hours

The challenge is that ADP and Microsoft Entra speak different languages and operate on different clocks. ADP exposes a REST API and SCIM endpoint. Entra consumes SCIM 2.0 for inbound provisioning. Bridging these two systems requires a connector layer that understands both, handles attribute mapping, manages error recovery, and provides a complete audit trail. This is precisely where Aquera and SailPoint compete for enterprise attention.

Section 03

The Connector Layer: Aquera vs SailPoint

Two enterprise-grade approaches to bridging ADP Workforce Now with Microsoft Entra ID have emerged as the dominant choices. Aquera's HR Sync Bridge targets organizations that want a fast, no-code path to automated provisioning. SailPoint Identity Security Cloud offers a full IGA platform for organizations that need governance, certification, and role management on top of provisioning. Both ultimately deliver SCIM payloads to Entra — but the journey and the capabilities differ significantly.

Aquera: No-Code HR Inbound Provisioning

Aquera operates as a purpose-built connector service between HR systems and identity platforms. It polls the ADP API on a configurable schedule, detects worker events (hire, job change, termination), applies no-code attribute mapping rules, and pushes SCIM 2.0 bulk upload payloads to Entra's /bulkUpload endpoint. The entire configuration — field mappings, department-to-access-profile rules, sync frequency — lives in a visual UI. No code, no middleware to maintain, no SCIM server to host.

Aquera HR Sync Bridge

Best for: organizations already invested in Microsoft Entra that want automated JML within days, not months. Aquera handles the connector complexity so IT can focus on access package design in Entra.

Key strengths: rapid deployment, native SCIM /bulkUpload integration, no-code attribute mapping, supports ADP + 50+ other HR sources.

SailPoint Identity Security Cloud: Full IGA Platform

SailPoint takes a broader scope. Rather than acting as a connector, SailPoint ISC is itself an IGA platform — it ingests HR data from ADP as a source, runs identity lifecycle policies, manages role engineering, triggers access certifications, and then provisions to Entra (and dozens of other targets) via MS Graph and SCIM. The governance layer — campaign-based certifications, separation of duties, role analytics — is where SailPoint earns its enterprise positioning.

SailPoint Identity Security Cloud

Best for: enterprises requiring access certification campaigns, SoD policy enforcement, role mining, and governance reporting — particularly those already running SailPoint on-prem (IIQ) and migrating to cloud.

Key strengths: full IGA suite, certification campaigns, role management, deep integration with HR sources, Entra provisioning via MS Graph + SCIM.

Section 04

Microsoft Entra IGA: Where Governance Happens

Once the connector layer delivers SCIM payloads to Entra, Microsoft Entra Identity Governance takes over as the policy engine. This is where the actual provisioning intelligence lives: access packages bundle together entitlements (group memberships, app roles, SharePoint sites) into named resources that reflect real business roles. Lifecycle workflows automate tasks — sending onboarding emails, generating Temporary Access Passes, triggering manager notifications — at defined pre-hire, on-hire, and post-termination milestones.

The SCIM provisioning engine in Entra then fans out to every connected SaaS application. As soon as an access package is assigned to a new hire, Entra begins provisioning the applications it contains: creating a Figma account with Editor permissions, adding the user to the GitHub org in the right team, granting a Salesforce Standard User profile, and provisioning a Slack workspace invite. Each of these uses SCIM 2.0, with the state of every operation tracked and auditable in Entra's provisioning logs.

Microsoft Entra Identity Governance — Core Concepts

Access Packages: bundles of entitlements (groups, app roles, SharePoint sites) that represent a named business role. A package called "eng-platform-starter" might include 8 app assignments.

Lifecycle Workflows: automated task sequences triggered by identity events — send TAP on day 0, notify manager 7 days before start, disable account on last day.

Access Reviews: scheduled review campaigns where managers certify whether their direct reports still need specific access. Critical for Mover governance.

Entitlement Management: the policy layer that controls who can request, approve, and receive access packages — including time-limited, guest, and cross-org access.

For Leaver scenarios, Entra's lifecycle workflows are equally powerful. A termination event from ADP triggers an immediate chain: block sign-in (accountEnabled=false), revoke all refresh tokens and active SSO sessions, remove from all access packages and security groups, trigger SCIM deprovisioning to all connected apps, transfer OneDrive and Teams data to the manager, and schedule a 30-day soft delete. The entire sequence can complete in under 60 seconds. The window of exposure shrinks from days to seconds.

Section 05

What This Means for Your Organization

The interactive demo above illustrates what automated identity lifecycle management looks like end-to-end. What it cannot fully convey is the organizational transformation behind it. Before automation, provisioning a new hire at a 3,000-person company could involve six separate IT tickets, coordination across three teams, and a 48 to 72-hour delay. With ADP as the trigger, Aquera or SailPoint as the connector, and Entra as the governance engine, that same workflow completes in under 60 seconds — with every action logged, every access decision policy-driven, and every entitlement auditable.

The Mover scenario is often underestimated. Most security incidents involving over-provisioning come not from new hires, but from employees who changed roles and kept their old access. A Mover workflow that automatically swaps access packages — removing the Sales Ops entitlements and provisioning the CS Engineering ones — prevents the gradual accumulation of permissions that security teams call "privilege creep." Automated access reviews, scheduled post-transfer, give the new manager a clean picture and a 30-day window to certify what stays and what goes.

✅ Getting Started — Recommended Path

1. Audit your current state: Map every SaaS app in use, identify which have SCIM provisioning enabled in Entra, and count orphaned accounts per app.

2. Choose your connector: If you need fast time-to-value with ADP, start with Aquera HR Sync Bridge. If you need full IGA governance (certifications, SoD, role mining), evaluate SailPoint ISC.

3. Design access packages first: Before writing a single SCIM mapping, define your access package taxonomy in Entra — one package per role family (eng-platform, sales-ops, cs-engineering) with clear ownership and review cadence.

4. Start with Leaver: Automating the Leaver workflow first delivers the highest immediate security ROI. Instant deprovisioning on termination closes the most dangerous gap.

The underlying principle is straightforward: your HR system already knows the truth about your workforce. The job of an IGA platform is not to replicate that truth, but to act on it — automatically, consistently, and with a complete audit trail. ADP fires the event. Aquera or SailPoint carries the signal. Entra executes the policy. And every employee, from day one to day last, has exactly the access they need.
